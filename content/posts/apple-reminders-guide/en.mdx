Do you know how useful reminders can be?

Not only can they remind you to do something at a specific time, but they can also breathe new life into those videos gathering dust in your bookmarks—and they’re easy to find. They can even help you pack your bags before you go on a trip.

My advice is to start by creating lists based on your needs. They don’t have to be perfect right away; otherwise, it’ll take a lot of time to organize them once you’ve accumulated a huge collection.

---

## Feature 1: Reminders

I use this feature whenever I forget which day to water my plants. You can set the frequency of reminders—for example, every three days, a few times a week, or specific days of the week. Once you check the box for a recurring reminder, the date of the next reminder will appear.

You can also set the priority level; the more important it is, the higher it will appear in the list.

---

## Feature 2: Quick Access to Videos You Want to Watch

When you come across a video you find particularly helpful, first tap the share button to copy the link. Then open the Reminders app, paste the URL into the notes field, and give it a name. Be as specific as possible when naming it to make it easier to find later.

If it’s a workout video, you can set it up using the first feature. It will pop up as a reminder at the time you set, and you can tap it to go directly to the video.

This isn’t just for workout videos—if there’s a skill you want to learn, you can use this method too. You don’t need to set a reminder time, and don’t check the box—checking it will make the item disappear.

We can also share lists with others, so everyone can add content to the list. As the data grows, you can also categorize it more thoroughly.

Now we have our very own database.

---

## Feature 3: Checklists

Take packing for a trip as an example. Create a new list, write down everything you need to bring, and save it as a template.

When packing, you can check off items as you go—once checked, they’ll disappear. Don’t worry—when you create a new list, just select your saved template, and everything will reappear. You can reuse it in the future.

---

These three features may seem simple, but when combined, they form your own personal data system. Start with topics that interest you, gradually collect information, and slowly organize it—there’s no need to make it perfect all at once.
