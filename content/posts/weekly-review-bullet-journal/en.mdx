## A Single Sheet of Paper to Track the Week

I write down everything that happens over the course of seven days on a single sheet of paper, using different symbols to distinguish between tasks, daily events, my thoughts, and so on.

I reserve the left column specifically for tracking my reading or the time I go to sleep. I use a small stamp to mark a book I’ve finished—this reinforces my sense of accomplishment and also serves as a symbol that helps me quickly capture information.

---

## Marking Weekly Small and Big Achievements

I learned this method from a book; it’s a way to accumulate evidence that “I’m actually making progress” and avoid focusing only on the negative.

This week’s small achievement: I made a new video—I’m no longer just someone who thinks about things but doesn’t act on them.

Big achievement: I built a tool that automatically adds subtitles to videos. I finished it on Friday, and I still feel happy just thinking about it.

---

## I Don’t Chase Likes

Although my videos get a lot of views—and I’m very grateful for that—I don’t measure my success by the number of likes I receive. That’s because those metrics are largely out of my control, and the dopamine rush they provide is fleeting. If I were to chase after that, I’m certain I’d end up feeling miserable.

So I focus more on what I’ve actually accomplished.

---

## Reflections on the Sleep Challenge

This week, I only managed to complete the “go to bed by 11:50 PM” challenge on two days, and even then, I barely made it. Last week, however, went quite well.

Looking back at my weekly plan, I realized that this week was mainly because I was too absorbed in video editing and some other things I’m really interested in. But last week’s schedule was pretty much the same.

I’ve found that once I get immersed in something, it’s hard to stop. Next, I need to figure out how to step back from that flow state.

Looking at my digital timeline, I can see that I’ve basically been creating content all afternoon and evening. I think I need to plan my time better—I didn’t have a solid plan for the video creation I recently started; I just let my inspiration lead the way.

Although this approach makes getting started effortless, I still hope to complete the 11:50 PM sleep challenge.

---

The value of reflection lies not in what you record, but in discovering what truly matters to you, and then finding ways to improve just a little bit in the next cycle.
